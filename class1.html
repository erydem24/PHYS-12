In creating my press-fit construction kit, I wanted to do something simple but with a flair. I was going to choose triangles, but I saw most people using triangles, so I passed on triangles. Then I thought about pentagons and hexagons, but given my relative incompetence and beginerness, I thought it would be quite the work. So I chose squares. But my squares were not just squares. I added a little flair by smoothening the edges and giving it a somewhat circular shape. Also I went for bigger pieces, just to be more different.

I started off by sketching a square with a 50 millimeter side length. Then I offsetted the sketch by 10 millimeters to the outside for the connection slots. After that I drew construction lines from the midpoints of the sides, then offsetting those lines by a parameter i defined as "thickness/2 - kerf". This would allow me to create slots that fit perfectly to each other, also putting the kerf into consideration. Kerf is how much material the laser burns when cutting. So keeping kerf in mind is important.


The parameters that I defined are now "thickness = 3.80", "kerf = 0.20" and "width = thickness/2 - kerf". Then I completed my sketching and started to add the 3rd dimension. I started the process by extruding my sketch by 3.8 millimeters, which is the same as the width of the cardboard. Then I filed the edges of the connection slots by 2 millimeters to get that smooth connection in. I then filed my squares with a radius of 15 millimeters.
